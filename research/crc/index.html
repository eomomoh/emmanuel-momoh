At the UC Davis Center for Regional Change, I contributed to the evaluation of the Yolo Basic Income Project, one of the first 100 guaranteed income programs in the United States. My work combined qualitative and quantitative research to understand program impacts on participants and communities.

Duties

  • Led a team to analyze semi-structured interview transcripts collected by Yolo County officials on the Yolo Basic Income Project.
  • Applied grounded theory by reading a subset of transcripts, identifying emerging themes, and creating a coding tree, which was applied to the full dataset using the Dedoose qualitative analysis tool.
  • Collaborated with the quantitative team to conduct inferential and predictive analyses on survey data using Python.
  • Created graphics, authored reports, and presented findings to Yolo County officials and at the Center for Poverty and Inequality Research (CPIR) Graduate Student Retreat, UC Davis (September 18, 2024).
  • Contributed to drafting and formatting two manuscripts for this study, both presented at the 2024 CPIR retreat.

Outputs

  • Poster: Yolo Basic Income Project — CPIR Graduate Student Retreat (2024)
  • Manuscript Draft: Qualitative Insights from the Yolo Basic Income Project
  • Manuscript Draft: Quantitative Outcomes of Guaranteed Income
